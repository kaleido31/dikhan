HISTORY OF DERA ISMAIL KHAN

Dera Ismail Khan, known as D.I. Khan, is a city in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan. It sits at the confluence of the Indus and Gomal Rivers on the west bank of the Indus. The name "Dera" means "camp" in the local language. The area around Dera Ismail Khan has been inhabited for thousands of years, with evidence from archaeological sites dating back to 3300 BCE. By the 7th century, the city was home to a diverse population that included Brahmins and Buddhists.

In the 15th century, the region became part of Derajat, a territory established when Baloch people were invited to settle by Shah Husayn of the Langah Sultanate of Multan. Malik Sohrab Khan Dodai, originally from Makran in Balochistan, was appointed to govern the area around Dera Ismail Khan. His son, Ismail Khan, is traditionally credited with founding the city, although historical records from Emperor Babur's visit in 1506 do not mention it. During the Mughal Empire, Dera Ismail Khan was part of the Multan Subah and served as a key trading hub along the route connecting Multan to Kandahar. Despite its strategic location, it did not achieve great prominence until the British colonial period. In the early 19th century, the original town of Dera Ismail Khan was devastated by flooding from the Indus River. Nawab Sher Muhammad Khan of the Saddozai clan rebuilt the city in 1825, relocating it to a safer area on a small plateau away from the river's main channel. The reconstruction was planned with input from architects brought from Punjab, who designed the city layout with separate areas for Hindu and Muslim communities.

Under British rule, Dera Ismail Khan became an important administrative center. In 1861, it was designated as the capital of the newly formed Dera Ismail Khan Division. The city developed rapidly, with the establishment of a cantonment area, civil hospital, and educational institutions supported by British colonial authorities. The population grew steadily, with Muslims becoming the majority after the partition of India in 1947, which led to the migration of Hindus and Sikhs to India and Muslim refugees from India settling in Dera Ismail Khan.

In more recent times, Dera Ismail Khan has faced challenges including terrorist attacks and instability, particularly during the period from 2007 to 2014 when the Tehreek-e-Taliban Pakistan conducted a series of bombings and attacks in the region. The city was also a target of the wider Operation Zarb-e-Azb launched by Pakistan's military against militant groups across the country. Despite these challenges, Dera Ismail Khan continues to be an important city in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, contributing to regional trade and development.
